Assignments for Wednesday, March 11

Personal Portfolio

In order to apply for jobs efficiently and have the greatest impact, you should create a personal portfolio website. Your website should showcase your work as a student at the Iron Yard, and include information about you, your experience, and your design and development process. It should also include contact information.

Since this website is about you, it is your responsibility to decide the goals and content of the site. Begin by answering the folowing questions:

  • What are the primary goals of the website?
  • Who is the target audience?
  • What information is the audience looking to find?
  • What style best reflects the personality you wish to convey?

Proceed with sketches and designs. Constantly check your decisions against the site goals.

Complete portfolio projects

Decide which projects (minimum 3, better 4 or 5) will be featured in your website. Refine and complete these projects, and collect sketches, wireframes, and early designs to include in the porfolio, in order to help tell the story.

Be sure to test your final websites thoroughly. Use this checklist.

Portfolios must be ready for reviews on Friday, March 20.
