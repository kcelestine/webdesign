Assignments for Wednesday, March 23

Demo Day Presentation

Select your strongest portfolio project to be presented at Demo Day. Prepare a 5-10 minute presentation that describes the design process, critical thinking, and technical execution that went in to the creation of your website.

  • Review your website to make sure it is ready for demo. Act on any feedback recieved in class or from Portfolio Reviews. Be sure to test thoroughly in all browsers. Use this checklist.
  • Collect assets for your presentation.
    • Photograph sketches.
    • Save wireframes as JPG.
    • Take screenshots of designs.
  • Create your slide deck. Use this template.
  • Write your script. Writing out everything you will say during your presentation will force you to carefully consider what you need to say, and make sure you don't leave anything out.
  • Write a short introduction for yourself to be used in the Demo Day brochure.
  • Save your website locally. There is always a chance that Internet access is not available, so plan ahead:
    • Save your files to a folder on your hard drive. Keep this special copy to be used for your presentation.
    • Remove any references to external servers, like jQuery CDN. Link to local files instead.
    • Install any Google fonts used in the site to your Mac, and remove the links to Google's servers.
  • Practice your presentation.

Demo Day is Thursday, March 26 at 6:00 pm. We will practice our presentations as a group at least once this week. Projects or presentations that are deemed unfinished will not be allowed at Demo Day.

Before your presentation:

  • Quit all instant messenger programs, Slack, email, calendars, etc.
  • Turn on "Do Not Disturb" mode.
  • Disable your screen saver.
  • Choose a neutral desktop wallpaper.
  • Open your browser, and load your website in a tab ahead of time.
  • Turn off any plugins, extenstions, or bookmark toolbars in your browser.
  • Open your presentation slides, and select the first slide.
